We are moving to the next phase of the West Hills Capital system: the Deal Builder.

Please read this carefully and implement only what is described here. The goal is to build a clean, dependable deal-execution flow that fits the real business process and stays consistent with how pricing works elsewhere in the system.

## Big picture

The Deal Builder is not just a form and not just an invoice.

It is a working transaction sheet that should be opened from either:

* the Leads page
* or the Appointments page

When I click a button on a specific contact row, the Deal Builder should open with that customer’s data imported into it.

This Deal Builder is where I:

* structure the deal live on the call
* retrieve current pricing
* build the product mix
* show the customer what the trade would look like if placed right now
* then lock the deal when they say yes

At that point, the Deal Builder becomes the source for:

* invoice generation
* deal recap email
* later, appending the final deal record into the Deals sheet

## Core workflow we are trying to support

1. I work a lead or appointment
2. From that contact row, I click a button such as `Open Deal`
3. The Deal Builder opens and imports the customer’s existing data
4. I retrieve current pricing
5. I structure the products and quantities
6. I communicate what the trade looks like if placed right now
7. If the customer agrees, I click `Lock Deal`
8. That freezes the live pricing and totals for the transaction and prepares the next outputs

## Important pricing rules

Pricing consistency matters.

Please follow these rules:

### 1. Spot pricing

Use the **ask price** for spot, not bid.

This should be consistent with how we fixed pricing elsewhere in the system.

For the Deal Builder:

* Gold Spot should use the ask price
* Silver Spot should use the ask price

### 2. Product pricing

Do not simply price products off the public website markup.

The Deal Builder should use the actual backend/API pricing source for products whenever possible.

This matters because:

* Gold Eagles and Gold Buffalos often have different premiums
* Silver Eagles have their own premium behavior
* the website is public-facing pricing
* the Deal Builder is execution pricing

So the Deal Builder must price the selected products from the API, not from a generic website approximation.

### 3. Pricing moments

There are two pricing moments:

* `Get Spot Price` = live quote building
* `Lock Deal` = final freeze of pricing and totals

If product-level live pricing is available from the backend, use it appropriately at these stages.

## Deal Builder opening behavior

The Deal Builder should be opened from the Leads page or Appointments page on the selected contact line.

When opened, it should import whatever customer data is already known, such as:

* full name
* email
* phone
* state
* lead ID
* confirmation ID
* deal type if already known

The goal is to eliminate retyping and keep the workflow clean.

## Scope for this phase

For now, focus on:

* Cash deals

* locking the deal

Do **not** fully build out IRA execution logic yet.

IRA deals are still relevant, but I can handle the nuanced IRA processing manually for the time being.

Important:

  * FedEx hold for customer pickup

For now, IRA-specific downstream processing can stay manual.

## Deal Builder buttons and their purpose

### Button 1: `Open Deal`

This is triggered from the Leads page or Appointments page.

Purpose:

* open the Deal Builder for the selected contact
* import the contact data into the Deal Builder
* prepare the transaction workspace

### Button 2: `Get Spot Price`

Purpose:

* call the live pricing/backend API
* fetch current gold and silver **ask** spot prices
* populate the Deal Builder spot fields
* set a `Spot Timestamp`
* populate or refresh product pricing for the selected products if possible
* support live quote building while I am on the call

This should be the “show the customer what the trade looks like right now” step.

### Button 3: `Lock Deal`

Purpose:

* represent the customer’s commitment moment
* freeze the deal using current live values
* write a `Locked Timestamp`
* freeze:

  * gold spot
  * silver spot
  * product unit prices
  * line totals
  * subtotal
  * shipping
  * total
  * balance due
* prepare the sheet for invoice generation and recap email output

This is the “we are doing this” step.

## Shipping logic

Shipping should reflect the real business process.

For cash deals:

* default assumption is FedEx hold location pickup
* home delivery can exist as an exception, but do not make it the default

For IRA deals:

* shipment goes to the depository, not the custodian

Also preserve the simple shipping fee logic:

* if the customer buys under 300 oz of silver and under 15 oz of gold, shipping is $25
* otherwise shipping is included / $0

## Product logic

The Deal Builder should support product rows with at least:

* Product
* Metal
* Quantity
* Unit Price
* Line Total

Product dropdowns should be used.

Metal type can be auto-derived from the selected product.

Please make sure the pricing logic is product-aware, not just metal-aware, because products like:

* Gold American Eagle
* Gold Buffalo
* Silver American Eagle

can carry different premiums and should not be flattened into one generic metal price if the API supports product-level pricing.
